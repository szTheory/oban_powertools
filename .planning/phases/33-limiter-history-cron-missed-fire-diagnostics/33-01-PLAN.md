# 33-01 Plan: Limiter History Projection

## Goal

Project limiter history that explains block, restore, reconfiguration, and pressure transitions without exposing an unrestricted raw-event stream.

## Implementation

- Add a durable low-volume limiter history fact store for operator-meaningful transitions.
- Record limiter facts from the existing `Limits` write paths for blocked, released, cooled-down, and reconfigured states.
- Keep the main history scope at the limiter resource level, with partition keys and eligible timestamps as supporting context.
- Build `LimiterHistory` as the domain read model and keep LiveViews limited to rendering its summary output.
- Route `resource_type=limiter&resource_id=<name>` through the shared forensic bundle contract.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Limiter facts explain pressure and policy-caused blockers distinctly.
- Reconfiguration history records before/after-relevant evidence.
- Restoration wording does not claim exact restoration unless it was observed.
- Limiter detail pages show a bounded history summary and forensic deep link.
